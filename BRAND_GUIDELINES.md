# Vows & Vedas — Brand Guidelines
### For Creative & Social Media Teams

---

## 1. Brand Identity

**Vows & Vedas** is a luxury destination wedding planning company. Every creative decision — colour, type, copy, imagery — must communicate:

- **Refinement** — unhurried, editorial, never cluttered
- **Warmth** — intimate and personal, not corporate
- **Heritage** — drawing on Indian tradition elevated to global luxury
- **Trust** — understated confidence, never boastful

The brand lives in the space between old-world grandeur and contemporary minimalism. Think Rajasthan palace meets Vogue editorial.

## 2. Colour Palette

### Primary Colours

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-------|
| ● | **Gold** | `#C9A234` | Primary accent — CTAs, ornaments, highlights, icons |
| ● | **Ink** | `#1A1408` | Primary text, dark backgrounds, deep overlays |
| ● | **Ivory** | `#FDFAF5` | Page background, light section fills |

### Secondary Colours

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-------|
| ● | **Warm Gold** | `#E87B3A` | Secondary accent, hover states (use sparingly) |
| ● | **Surface** | `#FFFFFF` | Card backgrounds, clean surfaces |
| ● | **Border** | `#EDE8DC` | Subtle dividers, input borders |
| ● | **Muted** | `#9A8F7E` | Secondary body text, captions, metadata |

### Colour Rules

- **Gold is the accent, not the base.** Never fill large areas with gold — it's a detail colour.
- **Ink on Ivory** is the default pairing for editorial content.
- **Gold on Ink** (dark backgrounds) is used for immersive, high-impact sections.
- Never use pure black (`#000000`) — always use Ink (`#1A1408`).
- Avoid bright whites on digital — prefer Surface (`#FFFFFF`) or Ivory (`#FDFAF5`).
- Overlays on photography: use `rgba(26,20,8, 0.35–0.65)` (Ink at varying opacity).

### Social Media Colour Combinations

| Context | Background | Text | Accent |
|---------|------------|------|--------|
| Feed post (light) | `#FDFAF5` | `#1A1408` | `#C9A234` |
| Feed post (dark) | `#1A1408` | `#FFFFFF` | `#C9A234` |
| Story/Reel overlay | Photo + `rgba(26,20,8,0.45)` | `#FFFFFF` | `#C9A234` |

## 3. Typography

### Typefaces

#### Cormorant Garamond — Display / Heading Font
- **Use for:** Headlines, titles, pull quotes, display copy, the brand wordmark
- **Available on:** Google Fonts → [Cormorant Garamond](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Cormorant+Garamond)
- **Weights to use:** Light (300), Regular (400), Italic (400 italic)
- **Never use:** Bold (700+) — this font should always feel elegant and light

#### DM Sans — Body / UI Font
- **Use for:** Body copy, captions, navigation, labels, buttons, UI elements
- **Available on:** Google Fonts → [DM Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/DM+Sans)
- **Weights to use:** Light (300), Regular (400), Medium (500), SemiBold (600)
- **Never use:** Bold or Black weights — keep it refined

### Type Scale

| Element | Font | Size | Weight | Tracking |
|---------|------|------|--------|----------|
| Hero title | Cormorant Garamond | 88–110px | Light 300 | Default |
| Section title | Cormorant Garamond | 38–68px | Light 300 | Default |
| Page hero title | Cormorant Garamond | 48–88px | Light 300 | Default |
| Sub-heading | Cormorant Garamond | 26–40px | Regular 400 | Default |
| Eyebrow label | DM Sans | 9–11px | Medium 500 | +0.5em |
| Body copy | DM Sans | 13–17px | Light 300 | Default |
| Button / CTA | DM Sans | 11px | SemiBold 600 | +0.22em |
| Caption / meta | DM Sans | 10–11px | Medium 500 | +0.2–0.4em |

### Typography Rules

- **Italic is expressive.** Use Cormorant Garamond italic for the emotional word(s) in a headline — e.g., *"Our* Services", "Wedding *Hashtag Generator*", "Where *Vision* Meets *Devotion*"
- **Eyebrow labels** are always: UPPERCASE · DM Sans Medium · 10–11px · Letter-spacing 0.5em · Gold colour
- **Never mix** Cormorant Garamond and DM Sans in the same line — they play distinct roles
- **Hierarchy is everything.** One dominant heading, one supporting label, supporting body copy. No competing type sizes.
- Maintain **generous line-height** on headings (1.05–1.15) and body copy (1.8–2.0)

## 4. Logo & Wordmark

### Wordmark
**VOWS & VEDAS** — set in Cormorant Garamond, Regular weight, letter-spacing 0.12em, uppercase or mixed case.

### Usage Rules
- Minimum clear space: equal to the height of the "V" on all sides
- On light backgrounds: use Ink (`#1A1408`)
- On dark/photo backgrounds: use White (`#FFFFFF`) or Gold (`#C9A234`)
- Never stretch, distort, rotate, or apply drop shadows to the wordmark
- Never set it in DM Sans or any other typeface

## 5. Ornamental Design Elements

The brand uses signature gold ornamental elements that should appear consistently across all creative outputs.

### Gold Wave Divider
A horizontal ornamental SVG — thin flanking lines, a central wave motif, and a small ring ornament. Used to open and close section headers.

- **Light backgrounds:** Gold (`#C9A234`) at ~90% opacity
- **Dark backgrounds:** Gold (`#C9A234`) at ~90% opacity, inner ring fill = Ink (`#1A1408`)
- Always appears **above** the eyebrow label and **below** the section title
- Do not stretch or recolour

### Corner Bracket Ornaments
Four-corner L-shaped brackets with a diamond terminal. Used on cards, stat boxes, and panel layouts to create a framed, regal feel.

- Opacity: 35–45% of Gold
- Size varies by container: 36–48px
- Always placed at all four corners of a container, not just one

### Section Wave Separator
A full-width ornamental separator between major page sections. Features a central wave with flanking horizontal rules.

### Use in Social Media
- Include corner brackets on quote cards and announcement graphics
- Use the wave divider to separate the eyebrow label from the heading in graphics
- Maintain proportional sizing — ornaments should never dominate

## 6. Photography Style

### Subject & Mood
- **Couples:** Candid emotion over posed stiffness — laughter, tears, quiet moments
- **Venues:** Wide establishing shots emphasising scale and grandeur; detail shots of décor
- **Details:** Close-ups of flowers, jewellery, fabric, hands — rich texture and bokeh

### Technical Style
- **Tone:** Warm, golden hour wherever possible; avoid cool/blue tones
- **Exposure:** Slightly underexposed is preferable to overexposed — preserve highlight detail
- **Colour grade:** Warm shadows (towards amber/sienna), de-saturated highlights, rich midtones
- **Composition:** Editorial — negative space, rule of thirds, strong leading lines

### Overlays on Photography
- Use Ink (`rgba(26,20,8, 0.25–0.65)`) to create legible text overlays
- Gradient overlays should run bottom-to-top (darkest at bottom where text sits)
- Avoid heavy vignettes — the brand feel is luminous, not moody-dark

### What to Avoid
- Generic stock photography
- Over-edited, heavily filtered, or artificially saturated images
- Cold or blue-tinted colour grades
- Cluttered compositions

## 7. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
**Poetic but precise.** The brand speaks like a knowledgeable, warm friend who also happens to have exquisite taste.

### Tone by Platform

| Platform | Tone | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| Website | Editorial, elevated, unhurried | "Crafting timeless ceremonies across the world's most extraordinary destinations." |
| Instagram feed | Warm, evocative, story-led | "Three days, a lake palace, and a love story that began in Udaipur." |
| Instagram stories | Conversational, behind-the-scenes | "Sneak peek from last weekend's mehendi 🌸" |
| Enquiry / email | Personal, attentive, professional | "We'd love to hear about your vision." |
| Captions | 1–3 sentences max, then hashtags separated by a line break | See hashtag section below |

### Copy Rules
- **Never use** generic wedding clichés: "big day", "tying the knot", "happily ever after"
- **Preferred language:** "ceremony", "celebration", "vision", "dream", "journey", "craft", "curate"
- Write in **present tense** where possible — it feels immediate and alive
- Use **em dashes** (—) not hyphens for pauses in copy
- Headings use **sentence case with italic accent** on the emotional word

## 8. Social Media Guidelines

### Instagram Grid
- Maintain a **warm, editorial aesthetic** — the grid should feel like a luxury magazine
- Aim for variety: alternate between couple portraits, venue/landscape, detail shots, and behind-the-scenes
- Use the **Ivory (`#FDFAF5`) background** for graphic/quote posts so they sit naturally in the feed
- Avoid posting more than two consecutive graphic posts — break with photography

### Caption Structure
```
[1–2 evocative sentences about the moment/story]

[Optional: couple names + location in italics or em dashes]

·

[Hashtags on separate lines or grouped below]
```

### Hashtag Strategy
- **Brand hashtag:** #VowsAndVedas
- **Content hashtags:** #DestinationWedding #LuxuryWedding #IndianWedding #WeddingPlanner #WeddingInspiration #BridalStyle #WeddingPhotography
- **Location hashtags:** add location-specific tags per wedding (#UdaipurWedding #RajasthanWedding #SantoriniWedding etc.)
- Max 15–20 hashtags per post — avoid hashtag stuffing

### Stories & Reels
- Text on stories: Cormorant Garamond for headlines, DM Sans for supporting text
- Maintain brand colour palette — avoid native Instagram colour tools that introduce off-brand colours
- Reels should open with a strong visual hook in the first 2 seconds
- Always include the wordmark or logo in the final frame

---

## 9. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Let photography breathe — generous whitespace is a design choice, not emptiness
- Use italic for emotional emphasis in headlines
- Keep gold as an accent — a little goes a long way
- Maintain typographic hierarchy: one dominant heading per visual
- Use warm, golden-hour photography whenever possible

### Don't
- Use more than two typefaces in a single design
- Fill large areas with gold — it loses its impact
- Use bright, saturated colours outside the palette
- Apply drop shadows or glows to the wordmark or ornamental elements
- Use sans-serif fonts for display headings
- Place text over busy photography without a proper overlay
